    Hengmin Zhang 张恒敏

    Zhang Hengmin, PhD, Professor, PhD Supervisor and a recipient of the National Young High-Level Talent Program, completed his undergraduate and master’s studies in Mathematics and later obtained his PhD in Control Science and Engineering from Nanjing University of Science and Technology; after his doctoral graduation, he first conducted postdoctoral research at the School of Information Science and Engineering of East China University of Science and Technology, and then pursued further postdoctoral research at the Faculty of Science and Technology of the University of Macau and the School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering of Nanyang Technological University. He has long been dedicated to the research on fundamental theories, key technologies and their applications in the fields of pattern recognition, artificial intelligence and large-scale optimization, with his main research focuses including: 1) the intelligent processing and analysis of visual information such as images and videos, constructing novel mathematical models and efficient optimization mechanisms driven by typical tasks, and systematically exploring the underlying computational foundations of artificial intelligence and new paradigms for perceptual reasoning; 2) the deep integration of theoretical methods such as sparse coding, low-rank representation and tensor learning with advanced neural network architectures to develop efficient and robust modeling and analysis methods for data in real and complex scenarios; 3) addressing the core bottlenecks such as low computational efficiency and poor interpretability in large-scale non-convex optimization problems, and constructing highly efficient and interpretable optimization algorithms and lightweight system frameworks with rigorous theoretical guarantees. His relevant research achievements have been applied to many important fields including complex scenario data processing, intelligent information system construction, industrial quality inspection and medical image analysis, and he has published more than 30 academic papers in top-tier CAS Grade 1/CCF Class A journals and conferences such as the IEEE Transactions series and Pattern Recognition.
